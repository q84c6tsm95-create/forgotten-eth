PresalePool 2018 +39 ETH
A 2018 ICO presale pool built on the Cintix-style PresalePool template — the same crowdpooling pattern that aggregated thousands of ETH for early ICOs whose minimum allocations were too high for individual contributors. The underlying presale was cancelled and the project's multisig refunded the raised ETH back to the pool, which was then placed into Refund state. Most of the 38 contributors withdrew during the original distribution window; 10 addresses never returned and still hold proportional shares totaling 39.20 ETH (one share is 10 ETH). The contract is unpaused and the participant set is frozen — claim is one call to withdrawAll() with no arguments, no token approvals, and no time locks. Identifying detail: balances() does not zero on withdraw, so live claimable equals balance minus cumulative paid-out; pre-integration verification reconciled the share-vs-onchain math (39.20 ETH each side) and Tenderly-simulated a withdrawal end-to-end.

Metadrop: Webaverse +134 ETH
In August 2022, Metadrop ran a sealed-bid uniform-price auction for the Webaverse metaverse NFT collection (7,870 items). Bidders above the clearing price won NFTs; the difference between their bid and the clearing price is refundable via a merkle-gated claim. Webaverse went dormant late 2023 and metadrop.com shut down. 90 bidders holding 134.15 ETH have never claimed their refund. The on-chain merkle root is frozen and matching proofs were reconstructed from archived Metadrop frontend JS bundles (Wayback Machine) after the live API went offline — each proof verified against the root and simulated claim-by-claim on a fork before integration. Claim is one call: claimRefund(refundAmount, proof).

X2Y2 Presale +28 WETH
Alongside the February 2022 X2Y2 launch, a signer-gated presale sold 1,000 staking shares at 1.5 ETH each (1,500 ETH raised). Presale participants earned a proportional share of marketplace WETH fees directed to this contract over a one-year staking window. Staking ended February 2023 and the x2y2.io frontend went offline. The contract is unpaused and immutable — 202 presale participants who still hold their X2Y2 token share can claim 27.54 WETH of pending rewards via harvest() at any time. Depositors who already called withdraw() fully-claimed their X2Y2 tokens and forfeited future harvests by design; the remaining 121 WETH is stranded in the reward-accounting math and requires the X2Y2 admin multisig (same 2-of-3 Safe as Fee Sharing) to sweep.

Nomad Bridge Recovery +100 WETH
Post-exploit recovery contract for the August 2022 Nomad bridge hack. The Accountant contract holds 100 WETH total, issuing one ERC-721 NFT per (user, asset) pair — covering WETH, USDC, USDT, DAI, FRAX, WBTC, CQT, and FXS. Each NFT is redeemable independently via recover(tokenId). Two requirements: the caller must own the NFT AND be on the contract's allowList. 178 addresses currently hold unclaimed WETH NFTs, with ~90% already allowlisted; non-allowlisted holders need to request KYC from the Nomad team before claim will go through. The allowList grows over time as new users are approved — the refresh cron re-queries allowList(addr) every 12h, so previously-gated addresses flip to "ready to claim" automatically once Nomad adds them.

Gnosis Auction +306 WETH
The EasyAuction batch-auction contract (2021) still holds unclaimed WETH from 565 auctions. Bids below the clearing price are refundable via claimFromParticipantOrder(auctionId, orders[]) — one call per auction, with order bytes packing userId + buyAmount + sellAmount. The gnosis-auction.eth.limo UI is dead but the contract is immutable with no deadline. Exact per-user refund computed by simulating each claim via eth_call: 305.91 WETH across 109 bidders, 99.4% of the contract balance.

Mesa / Gnosis Protocol v1 +139 WETH
The precursor to CowSwap (Gnosis Protocol v1), a batch-auction DEX deployed in 2020 and dormant since early 2022. 116 unique holders forgot to finalize WETH withdrawals after their last trades. Opyn v2 Gamma +380 WETH
First major decentralized options protocol on Ethereum, dormant since early 2022. Holders of expired in-the-money WETH-collateralized oTokens can redeem their share of the underlying collateral via Controller.operate([Redeem]). 2,441 unique holders across 38 expired oToken series. To unblock redemption, the missing USDC=$1 expiry prices were pushed to the Opyn Oracle for 23 historical expiries (a permissionless one-time action via the ChainlinkStablePricer). Each holder may have multiple oToken positions; the claim batches them into a single tx.
